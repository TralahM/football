How many goals are scored by the Home Team and Away Team on average?¶

Using the columns FTHG, and FTAG we calculate the average With dataframe indexing we apply the mean function

In [5]:
avg_fthg=df1718['FTHG'].mean()
avg_ftag=df1718['FTAG'].mean()
print("AVERAGE FULL TIME HOME GOALS: ",avg_fthg," AVERAGE FULL TIME HOME GOALS: ",avg_ftag)

AVERAGE FULL TIME HOME GOALS:  1.5315789473684212  AVERAGE FULL TIME HOME GOALS:  1.1473684210526316
  • Clearly we see the home team scores more goals frequently than the away team.
  • For every goal scored by the away team, how many goals are scored by the home team on average?
In [6]:
ratio=avg_fthg/avg_ftag
print(ratio," : ",'1')

1.334862385321101  :  1

Comparing Shots on Target with the number of goals scored in a game.¶

  • Let us now compare the number of goals scored in the season with the number of shots on target.
  • Is there a relationship between the number of shots on target with the number of goals scored in a game?

#### Let's Visualize these stats using a scatter plot and a line plot.
fig3=plt.figure(figsize=(13,5))
ax1=fig3.add_subplot(131)
ax1.scatter(shots_target_per_match,goals_per_match,color='r')
ax1.set_title("Scatter plot of goals scored vs shots on target ")
ax1.set_xlabel("Number of Shots on Target")
ax1.set_ylabel("Number of Goals Scored")
ax1.grid(True)

ax2=fig3.add_subplot(132)
ax2.plot(shots_target_per_match,goals_per_match)
ax2.set_title("Line plot of goals scored vs shots on target")
ax2.set_xlabel("Number of Shots on Target")
ax2.set_ylabel("Number of Goals Scored")
ax2.grid(True)

ax3=fig3.add_subplot(133)
ax3.bar(shots_target_per_match,goals_per_match,color='g')
ax3.set_title("Bar plot of goals scored vs shots on target")
ax3.set_xlabel("Number of Shots on Target")
ax3.set_ylabel("Number of Goals Scored")
ax3.grid(True)
plt.subplots_adjust(wspace=0.5)
  • Clearly we see that there is no evident relationship between the number of shots on target and the number of goals scored in this season.
